Data Driven Distribution

one of the things that the community was very happy we were doing was not only because of the food they got, but because we were able to customisation the kind of food we gave to pregnant women, small children, because we had the data. Where we did not have the data we gave people the standard ration. So I think knowing who are in the city what they eat for eg: even regular ration also were customised. People from Orissa and Bengal we gave certain kind of rice and Dal; people from North Karnataka we gave different food and then for people from Rajasthan we gave wheat flour - and people from South we gave a different kind of rice and Dal - we were able to customise to that extent because we knew the population rate.

Community Relief Efforts

Women's Health

The nature of the care chatline has changed over the lockdown, so during the whole time we were providing help for women, especially handholding around abortion and reproductive health questions

We also organised pad distribution because it was becoming tough at some point for people to have access to some basic services.

Sex Workers

During the lockdown, Sangama has raised money nearly 20 lakhs through crowdfunding for providing ration kits to sexual minorities and sex workers and we approached some other funders also. We constantly provided three months dry ration kit to across Karnataka, to around 7000 people - female sex workers and sexual minorities are our beneficiaries.

Waste Pickers

We worked with around 12,000 families of waste pickers - we gave ration for two months, and after that citizens approached us and asked us to help -approximately 50,000 families got rations for one or two months. Then we specifically worked with identifying people who have comorbidity who are on regular medication for pregnant women or children so we serviced them also. We knew this will happen so we started much early. In fact when they announced the lock down, that particular evening I think we had already supported 3500 families.

Domestic Workers

we have a group of around 85 senior citizen domestic workers, and we have adopted a granny initiative so we try to provide some food grains, rations and medical help to them as they are the worst affected now.

"If you are above 65, you are asked not to come to work and when that happens, you’re left with nothing - you know you have no other skill, you are at an age where you cannot do too much of work and then where will you get to work?"

Mobility During the Lockdown

The challenges were the difficulties faced in mobility due to the lockdown - there used to be some official vehicles and sometimes even people holding valid passes - BBMP issues certificates but the police won’t always accept it as the police also issues certificates and the police doesn’t issue certificates to everyone so you know there was a command failure at the government level at the administration level with regards to the validity of the certificates.

Dearth of Government Data

The MOHFW publish only today’s real time data, they don’t publish any archives of old data, so it was important for us to collect it every day - so that kind of put pressure that every day, at least initially, we had to keep looking at it to keep the data updated otherwise you would lose it - and the relevance of the data will also go away if you don't update it. That was stressful and of course, there is always some issue with data, right, they miss a zero at the end and things like that, so you had to manually check everyday.

" I think the way we collected data on the non-virus deaths itself is quite a shock because we thought somebody in the government would be keeping record of people dying through migration or walking home or hunger but at some point we learned that they were not doing it and that itself was a major shock. "

Online Education

You have a lot of big tech companies sort of substituting roles, so online education became this huge thing, where, because of the shift that happened overnight because the circumstances demanded it -- you suddenly have this situation where it became so much easier for Microsoft and Google and Byjus and all of these outfits to start pushing that, and these have long term impacts - the problem is not online education certainly, (I’m not an anti technologist) it’s about the fact that you end up privatising these solutions

Privatisation

I think looking at contact tracing for example or other kinds of like health data management these that was a learning was immense right like what was happening tells us more what should we really be looking for in terms of example if we look at a very simple thing like contact tracing, now all of these are technically in violation of many state laws like you know they do threaten privacy they do tend to have an overreach on citizen rights in many ways but most governments do have these laws that are about extraordinary times and pandemics are extraordinary times when they can take these measures and do these things. so that’s kind of an allowed thing it’s not like there’s anything wrong with that of course the way that it pans out is very problematic but one thing that you know a lot of people have been worrying about is that it’s very likely that the shifts that we see right exponential growth towards e-commerce like this you know interesting normalisation of surveillance all of these things are you know in roads to very long time shifts that we should be very careful about that how they pan out.

Smart Cities and its Shortcomings

There is very little focus on enhancement of access to public transport, not enough focus on clean transport like cycling, nor enough focus on how to encourage people to get out of the cars and get on to the public transport. With Covid, it has made it even harder - everybody is afraid to go onto public transport - the smart city has to be re-engineered entirely with the Covid focus

Smart City, it does not helps our community members, the Smart City policies. [...Have no hotspot, no closing fine, no closing fine for sexual minorities or female sex workers...] The Smart City concepts makes our community more vulnerable.
